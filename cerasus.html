Design and build an ergonomically considered chair that comfortably fits a range of body types — balancing structural integrity, material choice, and inclusive sizing within a seven-week studio sprint.

"To build a chair that transcends traditional design, evoking the aura of a throne and symbolizing power. With its innovative silhouette and swift armrests, it embodies an empowering blend of elegance and comfort, perfect for asserting authority in any space."
Design Statement
02
Inspiration

The Pipo by Piegatto

Sculpture as a starting point

Pipo by Piegatto — the inspiration reference

The Reference

Flowing lines, throne-like presence

Inspired by the flowing, sculptural rhythm of the Pipo, its seamless layers and sense of movement became a starting point for my own interpretation of form and balance.

The smoothness of the curves and swift lines speak calmness and elegance — but the form and mass of the chair give it a throne-like look, making it perfect for asserting authority in any space.

Pipo by Piegatto — A sculptural lounge chair known for its layered wood construction and organic silhouette, exploring the relationship between mass and lightness.
03
Research

How people actually sit

Observation as design input

Nearly every lounge chair we observed exhibited a slant from the front of the chair to the back. When sitting in these, we naturally slide to the back of the chair to receive the back support offered.

Viewing lounge chairs as a tool for relaxation helped identify certain traits and design criteria — reclining angle, distance from knee to back, and height of the seating surface all became measurable design constraints.

The initial sketches explored the relationship between mass and elegance — how a chair can feel both powerful and inviting. The key challenge was translating the flowing curves of the Pipo reference into a buildable, structurally sound design.

I started with ergonomics testing to determine the best and most comfortable dimensions — including reclining angle, distance from knee to the back of the chair, and distance from feet to the highest point of the seating surface.
